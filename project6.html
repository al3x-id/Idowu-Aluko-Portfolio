Business Insights

  1. BMW is the dealership’s top-selling brand, with over 3,200 cars sold, confirming strong brand loyalty and demand.
  2. German-made cars dominate sales, totaling more than 5,700 units, highlighting the dealership’s strength in premium European brands.
  3. Red cars are the least popular color choice, with only around 1,400 units sold, indicating limited customer interest in bold color options.
  4. Third-owner cars achieve the highest average resale price (~$80,775), suggesting that well-maintained older premium models remain attractive to buyers.

Strategic Advice

  1. Invest in expanding BMW and other German luxury inventory to capture existing demand and maximize revenue potential.
  2. Adjust pricing strategies or promotional campaigns to move red cars faster, such as discounts, trade-in bonuses, or limited-time offers.
  3. Promote the value of 3rd-owner premium cars as an affordable entry into luxury ownership, supported by quality assurance messaging.
  4. Prioritize stocking popular colors (black, white, grey, silver) to better match customer preferences and reduce holding time.
  5. Strengthen supplier partnerships with German manufacturers to secure exclusive models or better purchase terms, enhancing competitiveness.
